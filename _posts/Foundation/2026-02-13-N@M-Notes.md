On Night at The Museum, we presented at 6:50 pm, and overall, it went very well - we had a lot of fun showing off our game and talking about our process. We had a good turnout, and it was great to see so many people interested in our project. We got some positive feedback, and it was rewarding to see how much people enjoyed playing our game. ## What I Learned at the Other Stations:

I got to see a CSA and CSP's projects, and it was really interesting to see all of the different projects that people had been working on. I learned a lot about game design and development, and it was inspiring to see all of the creativity and hard work that went into each project. I especially liked CSP's project - it was a really unique idea to create a hub to gather all of the school's clubs, and I thought it was very well executed. CSA's cryptomining game was also very cool, and the shop and minigames were very facinating! Overall, it was great to see my friends' ideas come to fruition!

## Outside A101: 

Before and after our session, I looked at the other class's work - I always love checking in on Drawing and Painting, AP Studio Art, and Ceramics' work. They never cease to amaze me with their talent and creativity, and I always find something new and inspiring to examine. I took some photos of the art that I found particularly interesting, and I even found a like-minded artist in AP Studio Art who loves the same style of art that I do - it was really cool to see someone else who shares my artistic interests and to connect with them over our shared love of art. I'm very glad I got to see all of the amazing work that my classmates have been creating and can't wait for the nect Night at the Museum!
